About Us

To plant a garden is to believe in tomorrow

Gardens are all around us in some form or another.

Whilst the dictionary definition talks of “lawn and flowers” we like to stray from that restrictive notion and dig a little deeper. A garden can be a windowsill, an armful of pots on a rented patio and the tended and manicured ornamental design that hugs a stately home, providing inspiration on our day off. A garden can also be the collection of unidentifiable flowers painted by a 3 year-old that adorn the fridge, the self-seeded weeds that drive their way through the grey of a car park or the colourful print of a cotton blouse. They can take many forms; food, flowers, stone, moss - the possibilities are endless.

A garden is a place of contrasts; of physical graft and psychological freedom, of unfurling new lifeforms and delicate beauty in decay, they offer us a chance to escape from the world whilst being surrounded in a reflected, miniature version of it (with all of the pleasure and pain that mirror reveals.)

Forever a work-in-progress, this collection of gardens is ever-growing and with it we hope to focus on spaces of different descriptions to celebrate their uniqueness.
